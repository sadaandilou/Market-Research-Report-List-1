<p><p>The melt-spun fiber market is divided into two main types: filament melt-spun fiber and staple melt-spun fiber. Filament melt-spun fibers are continuous strands used in applications requiring strength and durability, such as textiles and industrial materials. In contrast, staple melt-spun fibers are shorter lengths suited for weaving or blending with other materials, often used in apparel and home furnishings. Both types leverage the melt-spinning process, offering versatility and performance across various industries and applications.</p></p>
