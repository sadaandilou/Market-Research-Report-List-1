<p><p>The protein biochip market is categorized into active and passive chips. Active chips are equipped with integrated circuits that enable them to perform advanced functions, such as signal amplification and real-time data analysis, enhancing their diagnostic capabilities. In contrast, passive chips rely on simpler technologies, primarily serving as surfaces for immobilizing biomolecules without internal processing features. Both types cater to diverse applications in molecular diagnostics, drug discovery, and personalized medicine, driven by the need for efficient and precise biomolecular interactions.</p></p>
