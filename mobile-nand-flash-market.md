<p><p>The mobile NAND flash market primarily comprises two types: vertically stacked NAND and photolithography NAND. Vertically stacked NAND, often referred to as 3D NAND, enhances storage density and performance by stacking memory cells vertically, allowing for greater data storage in a smaller footprint. In contrast, photolithography NAND, typically utilized in older technologies, employs traditional methods for crafting memory chips through photolithographic processes. Both types cater to the increasing demand for compact, high-capacity storage solutions in mobile devices.</p></p>
