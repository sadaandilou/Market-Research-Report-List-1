<p><p>The wheat seeds market is primarily divided into two types: winter wheat seeds and spring wheat seeds. Winter wheat is sown in the fall and harvested in spring or early summer, benefiting from cold dormancy. It's ideal for regions with milder winters. Spring wheat, on the other hand, is planted in spring and harvested in late summer or early fall, thriving in cooler climates. Each type is suited to specific growing conditions, impacting yield and quality in various agricultural contexts.</p></p>
